# RECON6 — Content Selling Kit
### Every clip should do one of two jobs: pull people in (reach) or sell the system (convert). The best ones do both.

Voice: direct, R6 vocab, no corporate speak. Casual emoji OK. Never oversell. The clip proves it; the words point to the offer.

---

## THE TWO LANES

**Lane A — Highlights** (3K / 4K / Ace, music, no coach voice)
Job: reach. The play is the hook. Selling happens in the *on-screen hook line* + the *description*.

**Lane B — Coach clips** (the AI actually calling a play)
Job: sell. The coach's voice IS the product demo. Keep music low or off. A short on-screen hook up top, the voice does the rest.

---

## ON-SCREEN HOOK LINES (first 1–2 seconds — this decides if they keep watching)

Keep it ONE short line, top third, big. Not a paragraph.

**For highlights (Lane A):**
- "My AI coach got me here."
- "This is what AI coaching does to your aim."
- "Ranked hits different with an AI in your ear."
- "POV: you have an AI calling your rounds."
- "Gold → Plat, with an AI coach."
- "No coach. Just an AI that watches every round."

**For coach clips (Lane B):**
- "Listen to what the AI just called 🎧"
- "The AI saw this before I did."
- "This is a live coaching call. Mid-ranked."
- "Turn the sound on — this is the AI coaching me."

---

## VOICEOVER LINES (optional — best on silent highlights, Lane A)

A 3–5 second VO over the opening of a highlight, then let the play breathe. Records in your voice OR I can generate it.

- "This is what happens when an AI watches every round you play. It caught the read, I took the shot. Climbed to Gold doing exactly this — link's in the bio."
- "People think coaching is aim drills. It's not. It's the AI telling you where to be — watch."
- "I built an AI that coaches me live while I play ranked. Three kills later, this is why it works."
- "Everybody's stuck because nobody tells them WHY. My AI does — every round."

Rule: VO sells for the first few seconds, then shut up and let the gameplay/music carry. Don't talk over the whole clip.

## THE RULES (so this doesn't drift)
1. **Never post a clip with a bad AI call.** A dumb call on camera sells against you. Human eye on every one.
2. **On-screen text stays SHORT and self-contained.** No mid-round jargon that needs context (no "plant in the back of garage" with no map). If a line needs the round to make sense, it's a caption, not on-screen.
3. **The play proves it, the words point to the offer.** Don't oversell in the video — let the gameplay be the proof, put the pitch in the caption/pinned comment.
4. **One clear CTA every time:** r6coaching.com. First session 50% off.
5. **Post daily.** Reach compounds. A good-enough clip today beats a perfect clip next week.
