// The One Doctrine

Siege is an information game that happens to have guns in it. Only ~0.4% of players ever touch Champion, and almost none of them got there on aim. They got there on information economy: every drone, cam, sound cue, and utility piece is a resource, and the team that wastes less of it wins.

Every tier below trains one layer of that doctrine. Skip a layer and you'll hit a wall two ranks later. Climbers average a 52.5% win rate — this is a consistency grind, not a highlight reel.

The Death Audit

SELF-VOD REVIEW PROTOCOL // RUN AFTER EVERY SESSION FROM GOLD UP
01

Did I die with info or without it?If you never droned or heard the angle that killed you, the death was decided before the gunfight. This one question diagnoses 70% of stuck players.

02

Was I tradeable?If no teammate could refrag you inside 2 seconds, you took a solo fight. Solo fights are coin flips. Champions don't flip coins.

03

What did my death buy the team?Acceptable answers: info, utility burned, time, a plant. If the answer is "nothing," that round loss is yours regardless of who whiffed after.

04

Did I have a win condition for the round?Before the action phase, you should be able to say the round plan in one sentence. If you couldn't, you were a passenger.

05

One fix, written down.One sentence, one habit, next session. Fixing one leak per session compounds faster than grinding 10 more games tilted.
