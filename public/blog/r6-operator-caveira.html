Caveira R6 Guide — Loadout, Sites, & How to Play in 2026

Defense Roam / Intel 3-speed / 1-armor 10 min read Last updated: 2026-05

Caveira is the silent roamer. Silent Step lets her sneak up on attackers, and her Luison pistol puts them in DBNO for interrogate — revealing all teammate positions. Plat+ players ban her for the information advantage.

This guide covers Caveira's loadout and gadget use, the maps + sites where they're strongest, common mistakes that hold Caveira mains back, counter picks Caveira is most vulnerable to, a practice drill to lock in their mechanics, and how to climb ranked with them. Loadout & Gadget Use

Caveira kit at a glance

  • Side: Defense
  • Role: Roam / Intel
  • Gadget: Silent Step
  • Primary: M12 / SPAS-15
  • Secondary: Luison
  • Secondary gadget: Barbed Wire / Impact Grenade
  • Speed / armor: 3-speed / 1-armor

Passive — Caveira moves silently when activated. Plus the Luison pistol that puts attackers in DBNO instead of killing, allowing interrogate (which reveals all attackers' positions).

Strengths

  • Silent footsteps for surprise flanks
  • Luison interrogate reveals all attacker positions
  • 3-speed mobility
  • Strong primary (M12)

Common Mistakes

What Caveira mains get wrong

  • Anchoring the same default position every round. Predictable to Plat+ attackers — vary your hold spots.
  • Wasting gadget charges in the first 30 seconds before knowing attacker setups. Wait for drones / commit reads first.
  • Picking Caveira on maps where another operator is mathematically better. Check the Best Maps table above.
  • Roaming when site needs an anchor (or anchoring when site needs a roamer). Match role to team comp.
  • Ignoring the secondary gadget. Most defenders default-pick — pre-plan whether you need barbed wire, nitro, or impact for the round.

Counter Picks — Who to Ban Against Caveira

If you're attacking against Caveira, the operators below directly counter their kit:

  • Lion (scan reveals Caveira even with Silent Step partial)
  • Iana (Gemini draws Caveira shots)

How to Play Against Caveira (Attacker Side)

On attack: bring Lion for scans — even with Silent Step, Lion reveals Caveira during scan window. Don't solo-flank — Caveira interrogate kills isolated attackers. If you go DBNO to Caveira, expect interrogate.

The general rule for facing Caveira: drone-up before commitment, identify their gadget placement, and either clear it (Twitch / Thatcher / Flores) or play around it (rotate, smoke cover, alternate angle). Forcing Caveira to react instead of letting them set up wins Caveira-affected rounds.

When to Pick Caveira — and When to Avoid

Pick Caveira when:

  • The team comp lacks their role (Roam / Intel) and the map favors it.
  • The map appears in the Best Maps table above with Caveira as essential or recommended priority.
  • Attackers are likely to use the strategies Caveira's kit denies (e.g., droning-heavy, gadget-reliant exec patterns).
  • You've put 50+ hours of muscle memory into Caveira — pulling them out cold is risky in ranked.

Avoid Caveira when:

  • Your team already has redundancy in Caveira's role.
  • The map is short or simple enough that Caveira's utility is overkill (a basic operator with a strong gun is better in those cases).
  • Caveira is banned (obvious) or the matchup specifically counters them (see Counter Picks above).

How to Climb With Caveira

Use Silent Step to flank attackers from spawn-side. Get Luison kills early in the round — interrogate reveals attacker positions for the rest of the team. Don't use Luison kills late round — too risky.

Tips for veterans

If you've been maining Caveira for 100+ hours, the climb above Plat / Emerald comes from the small refinements: rotating your anchor position round-to-round (don't hold the same corner more than twice in a row), pre-aiming the head-height angle attackers peek through your typical hold spot, and timing your gadget for the late-round commit rather than spawn-time pre-emption. The mechanical skills that got you to your current rank stop working at higher elos — opponents have read your patterns. Vary deliberately.

Practice drill — 5-game Caveira focus

Queue 5 ranked games with Caveira locked in. After each game, write down (1) where you used your gadget and whether it landed, (2) which map and site you played, (3) one mistake you saw in the kill cam. By game 5 you'll have specific patterns to fix, and the next 10 games convert that knowledge into rank. The deliberate-practice loop beats grinding 50 random games where you don't track what you're fixing.
