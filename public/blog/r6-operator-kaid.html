Kaid R6 Guide — Loadout, Sites, & How to Play in 2026

Defense Anti-Breach (Wide Range) 1-speed / 3-armor 10 min read Last updated: 2026-05

Kaid is Bandit's wider-range cousin. His Electroclaws electrify multiple wall sections + hatches simultaneously, making him easier to deploy on vertical maps. Stronger than Bandit on Coastline, Skyscraper, Bank.

This guide covers Kaid's loadout and gadget use, the maps + sites where they're strongest, common mistakes that hold Kaid mains back, counter picks Kaid is most vulnerable to, a practice drill to lock in their mechanics, and how to climb ranked with them. Loadout & Gadget Use

Kaid kit at a glance

  • Side: Defense
  • Role: Anti-Breach (Wide Range)
  • Gadget: Rtila Electroclaw
  • Primary: TCSG12 / AUG A2
  • Secondary: .44 MAG SEMI-AUTO / LFP586
  • Secondary gadget: Nitro Cell / Barbed Wire
  • Speed / armor: 1-speed / 3-armor

Three Electroclaws that electrify reinforced walls AND hatches in a wider range than Bandit batteries. Easier to deploy on hatches.

Strengths

  • Wider Electroclaw range than Bandit batteries (covers 2 walls if positioned right)
  • Electrifies hatches AND walls
  • 3-armor for tankiness
  • TCSG12 is a strong shotgun

Common Mistakes

What Kaid mains get wrong

  • Anchoring the same default position every round. Predictable to Plat+ attackers — vary your hold spots.
  • Wasting gadget charges in the first 30 seconds before knowing attacker setups. Wait for drones / commit reads first.
  • Picking Kaid on maps where another operator is mathematically better. Check the Best Maps table above.
  • Roaming when site needs an anchor (or anchoring when site needs a roamer). Match role to team comp.
  • Ignoring the secondary gadget. Most defenders default-pick — pre-plan whether you need barbed wire, nitro, or impact for the round.

Counter Picks — Who to Ban Against Kaid

If you're attacking against Kaid, the operators below directly counter their kit:

  • Thatcher (EMP clears Electroclaws)
  • Twitch (drone darts destroy them)
  • Maverick (silent breach bypasses)

How to Play Against Kaid (Attacker Side)

On attack: same as Bandit — bring Thatcher. Kaid's wider range means EMPs need to be placed precisely. Twitch drone darts destroy individual Electroclaws if Thatcher EMPs are limited.

The general rule for facing Kaid: drone-up before commitment, identify their gadget placement, and either clear it (Twitch / Thatcher / Flores) or play around it (rotate, smoke cover, alternate angle). Forcing Kaid to react instead of letting them set up wins Kaid-affected rounds.

When to Pick Kaid — and When to Avoid

Pick Kaid when:

  • The team comp lacks their role (Anti-Breach (Wide Range)) and the map favors it.
  • The map appears in the Best Maps table above with Kaid as essential or recommended priority.
  • Attackers are likely to use the strategies Kaid's kit denies (e.g., droning-heavy, gadget-reliant exec patterns).
  • You've put 50+ hours of muscle memory into Kaid — pulling them out cold is risky in ranked.

Avoid Kaid when:

  • Your team already has redundancy in Kaid's role.
  • The map is short or simple enough that Kaid's utility is overkill (a basic operator with a strong gun is better in those cases).
  • Kaid is banned (obvious) or the matchup specifically counters them (see Counter Picks above).

How to Climb With Kaid

Use Kaid on maps with multiple electrification needs — Bank (CEO + Tellers walls), Coastline (Hookah + Theater), Clubhouse (Cash + Stock). His wider range covers more positions per Electroclaw than Bandit per battery.

For specific site setups, check the per-map guides linked in the Best Maps table — each guide has the full operator + utility breakdown for that bomb pair. The interactive strats tool also lets you filter by Kaid to see all the strats they appear in.

Tips for veterans

If you've been maining Kaid for 100+ hours, the climb above Plat / Emerald comes from the small refinements: rotating your anchor position round-to-round (don't hold the same corner more than twice in a row), pre-aiming the head-height angle attackers peek through your typical hold spot, and timing your gadget for the late-round commit rather than spawn-time pre-emption. The mechanical skills that got you to your current rank stop working at higher elos — opponents have read your patterns. Vary deliberately.

Practice drill — 5-game Kaid focus

Queue 5 ranked games with Kaid locked in. After each game, write down (1) where you used your gadget and whether it landed, (2) which map and site you played, (3) one mistake you saw in the kill cam. By game 5 you'll have specific patterns to fix, and the next 10 games convert that knowledge into rank. The deliberate-practice loop beats grinding 50 random games where you don't track what you're fixing.
