Vigil R6 Guide — Loadout, Sites, & How to Play in 2026

Defense Roam / Anti-Drone 3-speed / 1-armor 10 min read Last updated: 2026-05

Vigil is the cloaked roamer. His ERC-7 makes him invisible to attacker drones AND Lion scans for 6-second windows. Strong against drone-heavy attack comps.

This guide covers Vigil's loadout and gadget use, the maps + sites where they're strongest, common mistakes that hold Vigil mains back, counter picks Vigil is most vulnerable to, a practice drill to lock in their mechanics, and how to climb ranked with them. Loadout & Gadget Use

Vigil kit at a glance

  • Side: Defense
  • Role: Roam / Anti-Drone
  • Gadget: ERC-7 Disruptor
  • Primary: K1A / BOSG.12.2
  • Secondary: C75 AUTO / SMG-12
  • Secondary gadget: Bulletproof Camera / Impact Grenade
  • Speed / armor: 3-speed / 1-armor

A passive that cloaks Vigil from attacker drones and Lion scans for 6 seconds at a time. Recharges between uses.

Strengths

  • Cloak vs all attacker intel (drones + Lion + Iana scans)
  • 3-speed mobility for flanking
  • Strong primary (K1A)
  • Cloak recharges — can be used multiple times per round

Common Mistakes

What Vigil mains get wrong

  • Anchoring the same default position every round. Predictable to Plat+ attackers — vary your hold spots.
  • Wasting gadget charges in the first 30 seconds before knowing attacker setups. Wait for drones / commit reads first.
  • Picking Vigil on maps where another operator is mathematically better. Check the Best Maps table above.
  • Roaming when site needs an anchor (or anchoring when site needs a roamer). Match role to team comp.
  • Ignoring the secondary gadget. Most defenders default-pick — pre-plan whether you need barbed wire, nitro, or impact for the round.

Counter Picks — Who to Ban Against Vigil

If you're attacking against Vigil, the operators below directly counter their kit:

  • Caveira (interrogate works on Vigil)
  • Pulse (heartbeat sensor still detects Vigil even cloaked)

How to Play Against Vigil (Attacker Side)

On attack: Vigil is invisible to drones — assume he's on a flank route. Use Pulse-equivalent intel (when on attack, doesn't exist) — instead, predict Vigil paths from spawn and pre-aim. Caveira-equivalent doesn't exist on attack.

The general rule for facing Vigil: drone-up before commitment, identify their gadget placement, and either clear it (Twitch / Thatcher / Flores) or play around it (rotate, smoke cover, alternate angle). Forcing Vigil to react instead of letting them set up wins Vigil-affected rounds.

When to Pick Vigil — and When to Avoid

Pick Vigil when:

  • The team comp lacks their role (Roam / Anti-Drone) and the map favors it.
  • The map appears in the Best Maps table above with Vigil as essential or recommended priority.
  • Attackers are likely to use the strategies Vigil's kit denies (e.g., droning-heavy, gadget-reliant exec patterns).
  • You've put 50+ hours of muscle memory into Vigil — pulling them out cold is risky in ranked.

Avoid Vigil when:

  • Your team already has redundancy in Vigil's role.
  • The map is short or simple enough that Vigil's utility is overkill (a basic operator with a strong gun is better in those cases).
  • Vigil is banned (obvious) or the matchup specifically counters them (see Counter Picks above).

How to Climb With Vigil

Activate cloak when you hear Lion scan or attacker drone deployment. Use 6-second windows for surprise flanks. Don't cloak preemptively — wait for actual scan / drone usage to maximize cloak value.

Tips for veterans

If you've been maining Vigil for 100+ hours, the climb above Plat / Emerald comes from the small refinements: rotating your anchor position round-to-round (don't hold the same corner more than twice in a row), pre-aiming the head-height angle attackers peek through your typical hold spot, and timing your gadget for the late-round commit rather than spawn-time pre-emption. The mechanical skills that got you to your current rank stop working at higher elos — opponents have read your patterns. Vary deliberately.

Practice drill — 5-game Vigil focus

Queue 5 ranked games with Vigil locked in. After each game, write down (1) where you used your gadget and whether it landed, (2) which map and site you played, (3) one mistake you saw in the kill cam. By game 5 you'll have specific patterns to fix, and the next 10 games convert that knowledge into rank. The deliberate-practice loop beats grinding 50 random games where you don't track what you're fixing.
