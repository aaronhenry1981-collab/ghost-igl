Defense Intel / Roam 2-speed / 2-armor 10 min read Last updated: 2026-05

Valkyrie is external-intel. Her Black Eye cameras can be thrown outside the site for spawn-peek reads. Banned in 60%+ of Plat+ matches because her intel + roaming aggression wins rounds.

Loadout & Gadget Use

Valkyrie kit at a glance

  • Side: Defense
  • Role: Intel / Roam
  • Gadget: Black Eye Camera
  • Primary: MPX / SPAS-12
  • Secondary: D-50
  • Secondary gadget: Nitro Cell / Impact Grenade
  • Speed / armor: 2-speed / 2-armor

Three sticky cameras that can be thrown anywhere on the map (including outside the site). Provides external intel for spawn-peek + flank reads.

Strengths

  • Throwable cameras give external intel (spawn area, rooftops)
  • Three cameras = three intel angles
  • Nitro Cell for area denial
  • Strong primary SMG (MPX)

Common Mistakes

What Valkyrie mains get wrong

  • Anchoring the same default position every round. Predictable to Plat+ attackers — vary your hold spots.
  • Wasting gadget charges in the first 30 seconds before knowing attacker setups. Wait for drones / commit reads first.
  • Picking Valkyrie on maps where another operator is mathematically better. Check the Best Maps table above.
  • Roaming when site needs an anchor (or anchoring when site needs a roamer). Match role to team comp.
  • Ignoring the secondary gadget. Most defenders default-pick — pre-plan whether you need barbed wire, nitro, or impact for the round.

Counter Picks — Who to Ban Against Valkyrie

If you're attacking against Valkyrie, the operators below directly counter their kit:

  • Iana (Gemini draws Valkyrie shots)
  • Twitch (drones destroy Black Eye cameras)
  • Dokkaebi (cam hack on kill exposes camera positions)

How to Play Against Valkyrie (Attacker Side)

On attack: drone every common camera placement spot before exec. Twitch drones can destroy cameras through walls (drone darts pierce). Don't expose to Valkyrie's known camera angles during exec.

The general rule for facing Valkyrie: drone-up before commitment, identify their gadget placement, and either clear it (Twitch / Thatcher / Flores) or play around it (rotate, smoke cover, alternate angle). Forcing Valkyrie to react instead of letting them set up wins Valkyrie-affected rounds.

When to Pick Valkyrie — and When to Avoid

Pick Valkyrie when:

  • The team comp lacks their role (Intel / Roam) and the map favors it.
  • The map appears in the Best Maps table above with Valkyrie as essential or recommended priority.
  • Attackers are likely to use the strategies Valkyrie's kit denies (e.g., droning-heavy, gadget-reliant exec patterns).
  • You've put 50+ hours of muscle memory into Valkyrie — pulling them out cold is risky in ranked.

Avoid Valkyrie when:

  • Your team already has redundancy in Valkyrie's role.
  • The map is short or simple enough that Valkyrie's utility is overkill (a basic operator with a strong gun is better in those cases).
  • Valkyrie is banned (obvious) or the matchup specifically counters them (see Counter Picks above).

How to Climb With Valkyrie

Throw cameras outside the site for spawn-area intel — read attacker spawns and rotation directions. Position one camera in the bomb room for plant-denial intel. Save 1 camera for late-round repositioning.

Tips for veterans

If you've been maining Valkyrie for 100+ hours, the climb above Plat / Emerald comes from the small refinements: rotating your anchor position round-to-round (don't hold the same corner more than twice in a row), pre-aiming the head-height angle attackers peek through your typical hold spot, and timing your gadget for the late-round commit rather than spawn-time pre-emption. The mechanical skills that got you to your current rank stop working at higher elos — opponents have read your patterns. Vary deliberately.

Practice drill — 5-game Valkyrie focus

Queue 5 ranked games with Valkyrie locked in. After each game, write down (1) where you used your gadget and whether it landed, (2) which map and site you played, (3) one mistake you saw in the kill cam. By game 5 you'll have specific patterns to fix, and the next 10 games convert that knowledge into rank. The deliberate-practice loop beats grinding 50 random games where you don't track what you're fixing.
