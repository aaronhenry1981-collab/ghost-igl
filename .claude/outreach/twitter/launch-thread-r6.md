## Engagement tactics

- **Reply to every comment in the first 4 hours.** Twitter algo rewards quick engagement.
- **Don't tag big names** unless they're already in your network. Cold-tagging triggers spam flags.
- **Quote-RT the thread from a separate account or your blog Twitter** if you have one.
- **Don't run a paid ad on the thread initially.** Organic only — paid amplifies a thread that's already working.

## Risk flags

- If the thread doesn't take off in 24 hours (under 100 likes / 20 RTs), don't keep replying. The tweet's dead. Try a different hook in a week.
- Don't argue with skeptics in replies. "Sounds like coaching grift" gets one reply: "Free tier exists if you want to verify yourself." Move on.
- Don't reply to bot accounts (followers <100, no real posts). Wastes engagement.

## Follow-up tweet ideas (1 week later)

- "Quick update: 47 R6 players have tried Recon+ since the thread. Most-flagged pattern across them: same anchor spot 4+ rounds in a row. Universal Plat habit confirmed."
- "Saved replies to the original thread asking about the Thatcher EMP timing. Posting the full breakdown today."

## Don't

- Don't ratio yourself by retweeting the thread 10x. Once is enough.
- Don't beg for engagement ("please RT!"). Cringe.
- Don't tag random journalists.
- Don't tease features you can't deliver. May 8 desktop app is coming — don't tease "AI mid-round calls" if that's not in the v1.
