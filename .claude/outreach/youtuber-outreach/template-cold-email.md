# Cold-Email Template — YouTuber Outreach

## Goal

Get small-to-mid YouTubers (50k-500k subs) to try Recon+ in exchange for honest review. NOT paid promo. Free Champion comp ($29/mo value) + transparent ask for an authentic take.

## Why this works

- 50k-500k subs is the sweet spot: too small for sponsorship deals, big enough to drive ~50-200 sign-ups per video.
- Honest reviews convert better than sponsored content. Their audience trusts them.
- Champion comp is genuine value, not a token freebie.

## Subject line options (A/B test)

- `[Channel name] — built an AI VOD review for R6 / multi-game, would love your honest take`
- `Free Champion access for honest review? Built something for FPS coaching`
- `Recon+ — multi-game AI VOD review, looking for skeptical reviewers`

(Recommend Option 1 — references their channel, lower spam vibes.)

## Email body (personalize bracketed sections)

```
Hi [creator first name],

I'm Aaron — built Recon+ (r6coaching.com), an AI VOD review tool that started as an R6 coaching service and now covers 10 FPS games (CS2, Valorant, OW2, Apex, Marvel Rivals, Halo, The Finals, CoD, Fortnite).

Why I'm reaching out: your [specific recent video — e.g., "Plat-stuck breakdown video", "VOD review of [pro player]", "ranked plateau analysis"] is exactly the kind of self-review thinking my tool tries to automate. Saw it and thought you might have the perspective to actually break the tool if it's broken.

The ask: I'd like to give you a free Champion-tier account (normally $29/mo, $0 to you, no strings) so you can run your own VODs through it. If you find it useful, an honest review video. If you find it bad, an honest review video. Either way, your audience benefits and I get real feedback from someone who reviews their own play seriously.

What you get:
- Champion access (all 10 games supported, full feature set)
- Direct line to me for any questions / bugs you hit
- No NDA, no script, no required posting

What I don't want:
- Sponsored-style promo. Audiences see through that.
- Positive review if you don't like the tool. Be brutal.

If you're interested, reply with your account email and I'll comp you the moment I see it. If not interested, just ignore — no follow-up emails.

Thanks for considering,
Aaron
r6coaching.com
@[your-twitter]
```

## Personalization checklist

- [ ] Use creator's actual first name (find on their About page or recent video).
- [ ] Reference a SPECIFIC recent video, not "your content." This separates your email from spam.
- [ ] Match game scope — if creator is R6-only, don't pitch the multi-game expansion in detail. Lead with R6.
- [ ] Spelling — quadruple-check the channel name and creator name.

## Subject lines to AVOID

- "Quick question" (spam alert)
- "Partnership opportunity" (spam alert)
- "We'd love to feature your channel" (spam alert + lying)
- Anything with emojis or URGENT or LIMITED TIME

## Follow-up policy

- **First email:** the version above.
- **No reply after 7 days:** ONE follow-up. Subject: "Re: [original subject]". Body: 2 sentences. "Hi [name], just bumping in case the original got buried. Still happy to comp you Champion if you're interested. No pressure either way." DO NOT FOLLOW UP MORE THAN ONCE.
- **No reply after second email:** Move on. The creator is not interested or not checking email.

## Reply handling

**If they say YES:**
- Comp the account within 24 hours. Send login email + 1-paragraph "here's where to start" guide pointing them to the VOD upload page.
- Wait 7 days, send a check-in. "Got a chance to try it out?" If yes, ask if they have feedback you can address before they post a review.
- DO NOT pressure for posting timeline. Respect their schedule.

**If they say "I'd do it but need $X":**
- Reply: "I appreciate the interest. I'm not running paid sponsorships at this stage — too early for me to commit budget. If that changes the answer to no, totally understand. Happy to revisit once we're profitable."
- Don't negotiate. Don't undermine the principle.

**If they ask "what's special about this vs [competitor]":**
- Be honest about positioning. "We focus on per-round positioning + utility timing flags rather than aim training. We support 10 games which most competitors don't. We're $9/mo founding rate which is cheaper than most coaching subs."

## Common objections + responses

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "I don't review tools without sponsorship money" | "Totally understand. No worries." (Move on.) |
| "I haven't played [game] in months" | "No problem — pick whichever of the 10 games you do play. Champion covers all." |
| "I don't trust AI tools to coach" | "Fair skepticism. The tool flags patterns; it doesn't replace human review. If you don't find it useful, that's the honest review I want." |
| "Send me a code first, I'll think about it" | Send the comp anyway. They might not post but they'll remember the brand. |

## Don't

- Don't email more than 5 creators per day. Spam filter risk on your domain.
- Don't email from a free Gmail. Use aaron@r6coaching.com or similar custom domain.
- Don't BCC creators. Each email is a 1:1.
- Don't include attachments. Plain text email only.
- Don't include UTM tracking links. They look corporate.
